Finding Internships.


These organisations offer several overseas placements and are one of the few possibilities available for work in developing countries.Positions for these type of organisations are generally designated as volunteer or service positions, rather than internships.

The organisations range from secular such as Amigos de las Americas or WorldTeach, to various religious-sponsored groups. Though several religious groups are insisting on affiliation, others, such as the Brethren or the American Friend Service Committee, do not find the idea of affiliation appealing.

Short term placements, which have a duration of less than a year, often charge fees, or at the best provide boarding. Very less long term placements provide for all the expenses of the volunteer, most notably the Peace Corps,which is an U.S. government program that funds positions with host country voluntary organisations.The possible benefit of the volunteer positions is the deferment of educational loan repayments during the volunteer assignment.

A Handy Tip - Visit the web site of the International Volunteer Programs Association ,www.volunteerinternational.org.Another useful information provider is the volunteer section of www.transitionsabroad.com,www.internabroad.com.Dont forget to check out the great book, "How to Live Your Dream of Volunteering Overseas by Joseph Collins, Stefano DeZerega, and Zahara Heckscher.
